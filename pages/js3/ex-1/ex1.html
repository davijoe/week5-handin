Q1: What is a promise?

A Promise is a JS object represents eventual completion or failure of an async operation.
It's a way to handle tasks that might take time, like fetching data.

Q2: What does the fetch method return?

fetch gives you a Promise that will eventually give you the server's response.

Q3: Why do we need to have two .then callbacks with our fetch code?

The first .then() turns the server's response into usable data. The second .then() lets you work with that data.

Q4: What can and should you do in the first?

In the first .then(), you make the server's response readable by turning it into JSON.

Q5: What can and should you do in the last?

In the second .then(), you use the data to update your webpage or do other tasks.
